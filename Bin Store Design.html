Lid clearance: With the front height at 1550mm and the wheelie bin at 1060mm, you have roughly 490mm of clear space above the bin. This is enough to flip the lid fully open (it swings back about 300mm above the bin top) and comfortably drop bags in without removing the bin.
Food caddy placement: The caddy (380×390×320mm) fits beside the wheelie bin if angled slightly, or in front of it near the door. Since you will open the door for the caddy more often than the wheelie bin, keeping it near the front makes sense. Alternatively, mount a small shelf at ~900mm height inside the left section for the caddy — keeps it off the floor and out of the way.

Construction Notes

Frame first. Build the skeleton from 50×47mm pressure-treated posts. Six uprights: two front (1550mm tall), two at the centre divider (height varies), and two at the back (1600mm tall). Horizontal rails at the top and bottom tie everything together, with the top rails creating the roof slope. Add mid-height horizontal rails on the left and back walls for cladding support.

Cladding. Shiplap boards fixed horizontally to the frame — left wall, back wall, centre divider (left-section side), and the front panel of the right section. The door is a separate frame of 50×47mm battens clad with tongue & groove boards, hung on two galvanised T-hinges.

Shelves. Two shelves in the right section (the bottom box sits on the ground). Each shelf is a piece of 18mm exterior plywood or several cladding boards screwed to 50×22mm battens fixed to the centre divider and back wall.

Roof. A sheet of 9–12mm exterior plywood sits on the top rails, overhanging 30–40mm on all sides. Cover with bitumen felt underlay first, then nail bitumen shingles on top. The 50mm height difference (back at 1600mm, front at 1550mm) gives a gentle drainage slope, with water running off the front edge where it drips clear of the door area.

Floor. No built-in floor — sits on paving slabs, concrete, or compacted gravel. If on soil, stand the posts on concrete pad stones to keep timber off the wet ground.

Finishing. Apply Ronseal Fence & Shed Preserver to all timber (including cut ends) before assembly, then a coat of Ronseal Crystal Clear Outdoor Varnish once built. Re-varnish every 2–3 years.
